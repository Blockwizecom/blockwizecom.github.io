Ethereum is a decentralized blockchain that allows applications and smart contracts to run continuously as planned eliminating downtime... Okay... so is it like bitcoin? Well yes in many ways bitcoin has the first and longest running blockchain and a lot of the properties out find in ethereum originated from bitcoin, that said though ethereum has some major differences and additions that expand on what bitcoin was created to do. Lets dig into ethereum and find out exactly what it is and how it runs.

Russian born Vitalik Buterin is the originator of the idea of ethereum which he put on a "white paper" Him, Mihai Alisie, Anthony Di Iorio, and Charles Hoskinson where the core ethereum team. The project was crowd-funded in mid 2014 raising millions for the project. So ethereum like bitcoin has a blockchain and there are millions of mining machines maintaining that blockchain miners earn ether as a reward for maintaining the ethereum blockchain and enabling the smart contracts to keep running.

Ether is the currency of ethereum also known as gas because it is used to fuel or pay for use of the ethereum blockchain for applications and smart contracts, the ethereum token is traded on cryptocurrency exchanges with the symbol ETH. The supply of Ether was projected to increase by 14.75% in 2017, with an algorithm in place to gradually decline to 1.59% by 2065(wikipedia) making the supply of ether tokens deflationary.

Onto smart contracts. Let's use an example, say a person purchases an option on the price of oil that it will be higher at a specified date than its current price, meaning if they are right they earn interest and if they turn out wrong they loose money, all the conditions and terms are put into a smart contract and the smart contract will execute as per the outcome on the set expiry date.
